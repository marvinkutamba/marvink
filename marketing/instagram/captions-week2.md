## Day 10 | Wednesday 18 March | Carousel
**Pillar:** Tactical Education
**Trigger:** Curiosity about tactics

**Visual:** Branded carousel. One tip per slide. Clean, readable, designed for saves and shares.

**Slide 1 (Hook):**
5 things your Instagram bio should tell a potential client.
(And what most bios get wrong.)

**Slide 2:**
Most photographer bios say something like:
"📸 Capturing memories | Canberra"

That tells someone nothing about what it's like to work with you.

Your bio is your 3-second pitch. Here's what it actually needs to do.

**Slide 3 - Thing 1: Who you photograph**
Not just "pets" but specifically. Dogs? Cats? Horses? All animals?

The more specific, the better. "Dog photographer" will attract dog owners. "Pet photographer" is vaguer and filters less effectively.

**Slide 4 - Thing 2: What the experience is**
Are you an outdoor photographer? Do you offer a full artwork experience? Is there a consultation involved?

Give them a sense of what it feels like to work with you before they even click through.

**Slide 5 - Thing 3: Where you're based (or who you serve)**
Location matters for local pet photographers. If you're in Canberra, say Canberra. Don't just say "Australia."

For coaches or educators, your service area is different. Make that clear too.

**Slide 6 - Thing 4: What to do next**
What's the action? Where do they go? What do they click?

One clear instruction. "Sessions enquiries: link below" or "Grab my free guide: link in bio." Not three options.

**Slide 7 - Thing 5: A glimpse of personality**
One line that sounds like a human wrote it. Not just credentials. Something that makes someone think "oh, she gets it."

Even just: "I believe your dog's personality is worth preserving forever."

**Slide 8 (CTA):**
When did you last update your bio?

Take 10 minutes this week and check it against these 5 things. Then come back and tell me what you changed.

## Day 12 | Friday 20 March | Static Post
**Pillar:** Problem Diagnosis
**Trigger:** Confusion/overwhelm
**Stage:** Problem-aware
**Visual:** Clean, direct. Text-forward or Ina looking thoughtful/direct.

---

Posting to Instagram isn't a marketing strategy.

It's a visibility tactic inside a marketing strategy.

There's a difference, and I think a lot of us have been conflating the two for a long time.

A marketing strategy asks: who am I trying to reach, what do I want them to understand, and how do I move them from "never heard of you" to "I want to book a session"?

Instagram is one channel that can support that. But it only works when it connects to something else: an email list, a lead magnet, a follow-up sequence, a clear offer.

Without those things underneath it, you're building a beautiful Instagram gallery that doesn't lead anywhere.

The good news is you don't need to have all of it at once. But you do need to know what you're building toward.

What's currently sitting underneath your Instagram? Where do you send people when they're interested? Tell me in the comments.
