# Instagram Content Strategy: Pet Photographers Collective
**Brand:** Ina J Photography / Pet Photographers Collective
**Audience:** Pet photographers (global) struggling with inconsistent bookings
**Goal:** Educate and sell. Build trust, demonstrate expertise, nurture enquiries toward the Collective, workshops, and mentorship.
**Posting frequency:** Daily (1 post per day)
**Strategy period:** 30 days (reference calendar: 9 March 2026 onwards)

---

## The Core Problem This Strategy Solves

Pet photographers in Ina's audience are talented. Their work is good. But their marketing is scattered, their messaging is unclear, and their bookings are unpredictable. They've tried giveaways, charity auctions, and sporadic Instagram posts. They're tired of advice that doesn't apply to their specific model.

This Instagram strategy builds a content presence that:
- Names their exact problem (often before they can name it themselves)
- Teaches practical frameworks they can apply immediately
- Demonstrates that Ina gets it because she runs a real studio
- Moves people toward the free Skool community and from there to paid offers

---

## Five Content Pillars

### 1. Problem Diagnosis
Naming the struggle with precision. When content reflects exactly what someone is experiencing, they feel seen. This is the most powerful type of hook content.
- "Good work plus random marketing equals random bookings. Every single time."
- "The 3 gaps: visibility, message, or conversion. Which one is actually holding you back?"
- Pattern-interrupt posts that name the invisible problem

### 2. Mindset Reframes
Shifting the way pet photographers think about selling, positioning, and their worth. Not inspirational fluff. Practical mental shifts with a clear "so what" attached.
- Selling is service, not pushing
- "It's not a sales problem, it's a positioning problem"
- Imposter syndrome as normal, staying stuck as optional

### 3. Behind the Scenes / Credibility
Ina sharing real numbers, real stories, and real mistakes from her own business. This is the content that makes her trustworthy rather than just credible on paper.
- Stories from her pet photography studio (client bookings, the email moment, Mac's story)
- Real dollar amounts: $2,600 from a reel, 140 sessions in one year, the $20K investment
- Self-deprecating honesty: showing up imperfectly, not liking how she looks on camera

### 4. Community and Social Proof
Validation that others are in the same situation. Member wins (anonymised). Engagement prompts that draw out honest participation. The "you're not alone" feeling that makes people want to stick around.
- Poll-style posts (emoji drop, yes/no questions)
- Member win spotlight with lesson attached
- "Be honest with me" prompts that invite real answers

### 5. Tactical Education
Specific, actionable content that teaches one thing clearly. Frameworks, systems, and processes that can be applied this week. Not theory. The "here's how this actually works" content.
- The 3 gaps framework in practice
- What a weekly marketing rhythm looks like
- Lead magnets, email systems, enquiry-to-booking process

## Four Weekly Themes (Month 1)

### Week 1 (Mar 9-15): "Random marketing, random results"
Introduce the core problem: good work is not enough if the marketing is scattered. Introduce the 3 gaps framework as the diagnostic tool. Begin building trust by naming what they already feel but can't articulate.

### Week 2 (Mar 16-22): "Your message is your marketing"
Position messaging and positioning as the real lever. Show that Instagram isn't a strategy by itself. Demonstrate what it looks like when your content filters for the right clients before they ever enquire.

### Week 3 (Mar 23-29): "Selling is service"
Address the biggest emotional block: selling discomfort. Reframe selling as guidance. Teach the enquiry-to-booking journey. Use the client email story to show why showing up consistently matters.

### Week 4 (Mar 30-Apr 7): "Simple systems, not more volume"
Teach the weekly rhythm. Show what consistent marketing looks like in practice (not posting every day, but having a repeatable structure). Move toward the soft offer for the Collective.

## Caption Structure

### For Static Posts and Carousels
1. **Hook (line 1):** Must stop the scroll. Statement, question, or specific scenario. No warm-up.
2. **Body:** Story first, then lesson. Short paragraphs. White space. 1-3 sentences each.
3. **Takeaway:** The one thing they're walking away with.
4. **CTA:** Soft invitation. Question, comment prompt, or DM nudge. Never a demand.

### For Reels (caption accompaniment)
- Captions are secondary to the video content.
- Keep them brief: 2-4 short paragraphs max.
- Expand on one point from the video.
- End with an engagement question.

## Posting Time Recommendations

Based on audience demographics (global, predominantly women, mix of day jobs and full-time business):

- **Primary posting window:** 7:00-9:00 AM local time (catches people before the work day)
- **Secondary window:** 12:00-1:00 PM (lunch scroll)
- **Evening option:** 7:00-9:00 PM (after dinner, after kids are in bed)

Because the audience is global (Australia, US, Canada, UK), aim for morning AEST which lands in the previous evening for North American audiences. This covers both markets.

**Best days for reach:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Friday
**Best days for engagement:** Monday, Thursday, Sunday

## Story Content (Supplementary)

Stories support the feed but are not the focus of this strategy. Use them to:
- Tease upcoming posts ("new post going up today about something I see happening all the time in the community")
- Share poll stickers aligned with the weekly theme
- Reshare feed posts 24-48 hours after publishing
- Show the real behind-the-scenes: editing days, session prep, typing a new email
- Drive traffic to the free Skool community (link in bio / link sticker)

---

## Offer Mentions (Frequency and Approach)

This strategy follows a 4:1 ratio: four value posts for every one post that mentions an offer.

Offers are always positioned by transformation:
- Skool community (free): "A community of pet photographers who get it"
- Consistent Bookings Workshop: "The system for consistent bookings"
- Mentorship/Collective: "What it looks like to have a structured plan instead of guessing"

Never: "Spots are filling up!" / "Last chance!" / "Invest in yourself now!"

Always: "If any of this felt familiar, the link in my bio takes you to where we go deeper on this."
