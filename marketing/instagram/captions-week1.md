Good work plus random marketing equals random bookings. Every single time.

It's not a talent problem. Pet photographers who are struggling to fill their calendars are some of the most skilled, most dedicated creatives I know.

It all just sits there, disconnected.

That's what I'll be talking about this week.

**Slide 1 (Hook):**
The 3 gaps that explain almost every booking slump.

**Slide 2:**
When bookings are inconsistent, it's almost always one of three things.

Not luck. Not the economy. Not your prices.

One of three gaps.

**Slide 3 - Gap 1: Visibility**
Not enough people know you exist.

You're not showing up consistently enough for new people to find you. Your content isn't reaching beyond your existing network. Your name isn't coming up when someone in your area is looking for a pet photographer.

If your last 3 clients all came from referrals or people who already knew you, this is probably your gap.

**Slide 4 - Gap 2: Message**
People find you, but your content doesn't connect.

Your photos are beautiful, but your Instagram doesn't tell people what you actually offer, why it's worth the investment, or what makes working with you different.

If people visit your profile and don't enquire, this might be your gap.

**Slide 5 - Gap 3: Conversion**
People enquire, but they don't book.

They ask for pricing and then go quiet. They seem interested during the consultation but you never hear back. Something is happening between "interested" and "confirmed booking."

If you get enquiries but they don't convert, this is your gap.

**Slide 6 (CTA):**
Most photographers try to fix all three at once. That's why they stay stuck.

The real move is to diagnose which gap is your main issue right now, then fix that one first.

Be honest with me.

Where did your last 3 clients come from?

I ask this in our community a lot because it's one of the most useful questions you can ask yourself. Most people don't know the answer off the top of their head, which is actually pretty revealing.

Because if you can't trace where your clients are coming from, you can't build a system around it.

Here's what I hear most often:

- Word of mouth / a friend
- Someone who's been following for a while
- A past client who came back
- Someone from a charity auction or giveaway

All of these are real, valid ways to get clients. But they're also mostly passive. They rely on other people doing the work for you.

Nothing wrong with referrals. They're wonderful. But you can't build a predictable business on something you can't control.

## Day 6 | Saturday 14 March | Static Post
**Pillar:** Mindset Reframe
**Trigger:** Permission and validation
**Stage:** Problem-aware
**Visual:** Warm, human photo of Ina or a simple text-on-background graphic. Nothing too polished.

---

You're not all over the place because you're bad at business.

You're all over the place because you're trying to do everything without a clear sense of what to focus on first.

There's a difference.

One of the most common things I hear from pet photographers is some version of "I'm all over the place half the time, like a dog in a room of squirrels." (Someone in our community said that and I think about it constantly.)

That feeling? It's not a personality flaw. It's a systems problem.

When there's no structure telling you what to work on this week, your brain defaults to what feels most urgent, or most interesting, or most visible. Which means you start a lot of things and finish very few.

The answer isn't to try harder. It's to narrow down.

What's the ONE lever that, if you pulled it, would move the needle most in your business right now?

That's where to start. Just one thing.

What's yours? I'd love to know.

## Day 7 | Sunday 15 March | Carousel
**Pillar:** Tactical Education
**Trigger:** Curiosity about tactics
**Stage:** Problem-aware
**Visual:** Clean carousel. Illustrative graphics for each type of marketing would work well here.

**Slide 1 (Hook):**
Reactive marketing vs. proactive marketing.
Most pet photographers are doing one. Very few are doing both.

**Slide 2:**
Reactive marketing is responding to what comes to you.

Someone refers a client. You get a DM. You run a giveaway and hope the right people enter.

There's nothing wrong with this. It's how most photographers start. But it's unpredictable. You can't plan your income around it.

**Slide 3:**
Proactive marketing is building systems that work consistently, whether you're in the mood or not.

Email list. Lead magnet. Weekly content rhythm. A follow-up process after every enquiry.

These aren't flashy. But they're reliable.

**Slide 4:**
The goal isn't to replace reactive marketing with proactive. It's to have both.

Referrals and word of mouth are still valuable. But they work much better when there's a proactive system underneath, backing them up.

**Slide 5:**
Here's a simple question to work out which one you're relying on:

If you went completely quiet on social media for 30 days, what would happen to your enquiries?

If the answer is "they'd dry up completely" — that's reactive.

If you have an email list, a lead magnet, and a follow-up sequence, they'd probably keep coming in — that's proactive.

**Slide 6 (CTA):**
You don't need to build everything at once. You just need to know which side needs work.

What's one proactive marketing thing you've been meaning to set up? Drop it below.
