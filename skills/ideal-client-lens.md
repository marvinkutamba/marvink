# Ideal Client Lens

This skill ensures all coaching content is deeply aligned with the ideal client avatar for Ina J Photography's coaching business. It acts as an active guide during content creation, not just background context.

---

## Content Creation Workflow

Every piece of coaching content should pass through these five checkpoints. Apply them naturally as you create.

### Checkpoint 1: Who Is This For?

| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Early** | Only offering digitals, no systems, clients from personal network only |
| **Mid** | Getting some bookings but inconsistent, sales average below target, tried some marketing |
| **Established** | Years of experience, awards, some high-spend clients, but no structured system for consistency |

Pick a primary stage and speak to them. The hook can cast a wide net, but the body and action step should land for a specific stage.

### Checkpoint 2: Which Buyer Journey Stage?

| Stage | Content goal | Content style |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| **Unaware / Lurking** | Name their problem before they can | Story-led, pattern-interrupt, "is this you?" |
| **Problem-Aware** | Give language and framework to their struggle | Diagnostic, educational, the 3 gaps framework |
| **Solution-Aware** | Show why this approach is different | Comparison, social proof, specificity of pet photography niche |
| **Product-Aware** | Remove friction and make next step feel safe | Testimonials, "here's what's inside", low-risk entry points |
| **Active Member** | Drive action, celebrate wins, maintain engagement | Action tasks, accountability, community energy |

### Checkpoint 3: Which Emotional Trigger?

- **Confusion/overwhelm** "Start here, then do this"
- **Ambition/pride in craft** "You're capable of this"
- **Frustration with wrong-fit clients** "This is fixable"
- **Curiosity about specific tactics** "Here's how this works"
- **Fear of falling behind** "Others are doing this already"
- **Legacy and meaning** "This work matters"
- **Permission and validation** "You're not alone / it's okay"
- **Seeing their problem diagnosed** "This is exactly what's happening"

### Checkpoint 4: Does It Pass the Avatar Test?

1. Would members comment "this is me" or "I needed to hear this"?
2. Does it use their language? Mirror how they actually talk about their struggles.
3. Is there a clear, doable action? One thing they can take this week, not five.
4. Does it respect their constraints? Day jobs, families, editing backlogs.
5. Is it specific to pet photography? Generic advice is what they've already heard and rejected.
6. Does it avoid their red flags? No fluff without framework. No volume/discount/mini-session advice.

### Checkpoint 5: What's the Internal Dialogue?

- **Pre-click:** Will they open this? Does the hook match what they're feeling right now?
- **First scan:** Will they keep reading? Does it feel relevant within the first few lines?
- **Mid-read:** Are they having an "aha" moment? Are they seeing their own situation reflected?
- **At the action:** Does the next step feel doable and specific?
- **At the offer (if applicable):** Does it feel like a natural continuation, not a bait-and-switch?

## Ideal Client Avatar: Pet Photographers Collective

### Snapshot

They are a serious pet photographer (or emerging "want-to-be-full-time") who already cares deeply about artwork, experience and legacy, but feels their business is not reflecting their talent yet. They may have paying clients and gear, but their bookings are inconsistent, their marketing is scattered, and they are tired of piecing together advice from random podcasts, Facebook groups and reels.

**Demographics:** Predominantly women, mix of full-time and part-time photographers (many still juggling day jobs or family). Located across English-speaking countries (Australia, US, Canada, UK, Europe). Range from newer photographers battling imposter syndrome to experienced photographers (10+ years) who still lack business systems.

### Core Pain Points (ranked by frequency)

**1. Inconsistent Bookings and Leads**
The single most common pain point. They don't know where their next client is coming from.
- "Consistent leads and bookings" stated almost verbatim by multiple members
- "Getting butts IN THE DOOR. My overhead is high and the economy is challenging."
- Many rely on reactive methods (charity auctions, giveaways, word of mouth) rather than proactive systems

**2. Time Management and Overwhelm**
- "I'm all over the place half the time like a dog in a room of squirrels."
- "I tend to sit down to edit and get sidelined by shinier projects."
- They procrastinate on business tasks because creative work feels safer

**3. Not Knowing What to Focus On**
- "Not knowing what to focus on" ranks high in polls
- They start many things and finish few

**4. Selling and Conversion Struggles**
- "I don't like chasing and being salesy. That's not my thing."
- "A lot of them message straight away asking for pricing and once I've given pricing a lot of them drop off."

**5. Digital-Only Clients and Price Pushback**
- "The digital-only crowd is my biggest challenge."
- "Everyone wants digitals and even with beautiful wall art samples I'm just not great at selling!"

**6. Imposter Syndrome and Loss of Motivation**
- "I still have that nagging 'I'm not good enough' in my head."
- "I'm tired and out of ideas. I struggle daily with the idea of just giving up the business."

**7. Pricing Confidence**
- "I've been selling myself short. My hourly rate was so cheap that I barely made anything."
- "Wondering if I should go cheaper to get more clients."

**8. Messaging and Positioning**
- "I'm a bit flip-flopping in how to position myself. Trying to still please too many people."

### Hopes, Goals and Aspirations

- Diagnose their own gaps (visibility, message, conversion) and know exactly which lever to pull
- Show up weekly with marketing that aligns with their positioning
- Run simple, repeatable systems: lead magnets, email sequences, nurture content
- Attract value-led clients who care about artwork, experience and legacy
- Feel in control of bookings. Able to predict roughly how many enquiries are coming in.
- See selling as guiding and serving, not pushing or being sleazy
- Work less, earn more by working with ideal clients at higher averages

### Fears and Concerns

- **Fear of shallow advice:** Tired of inspirational quotes and "post more on Instagram" with no practical breakdown
- **Mistrust from past investments:** "Please don't be another fluffy newsletter"
- **Fear of wasted time:** Limited capacity and worry that joining another workshop will add tasks instead of simplifying
- **Concern about misaligned advice:** Wary of coaches who push volume, discounts, or digitals-only strategies
- **Doubt around ability to implement:** Fear they will fall behind or "be the one person who still can't make it work"
- **Fear of AI replacing them:** "I honestly feel that people value what we do less and less."

### Emotional Triggers

- **Confusion and overwhelm:** When they realise they are "wandering haphazardly" and need someone to say "Start here"
- **Ambition and pride in craft:** Seeing peers share wins. "I know I'm capable of that too."
- **Frustration with misaligned clients:** Pushback on price, digital-only requests, ghosting after consults
- **Curiosity about specific tactics:** Lead magnets, email nurture, AI helpers, sales psychology
- **Fear of falling behind peers:** Members sharing wins create constructive FOMO
- **Emotional stories about legacy:** Reminders that clients later lose their pets and are deeply grateful
- **Permission and validation:** "It's okay to charge this" / "You're not alone in this struggle"
- **Seeing their exact problem diagnosed:** "Every time I read her emails or posts, I see my exact problems explained clearly."

### What Makes Them Disengage

- Long, vague content that restates "believe in yourself" without frameworks or actions
- Advice leaning into discounts, mini-sessions as the main model, or volume strategies
- Overwhelming volume with multiple CTAs and new ideas every week
- Every email turning into a pitch with minimal standalone value

**Their unsubscribe dialogue:** "I like her and the topic is relevant, but I am drowning in ideas. If the emails do not give me a clear, realistic next step for my situation, I might as well reduce the noise."

## Voice of Customer: Real Language

Mirror these words and phrases in hooks, body copy, and calls to action.

**Inconsistent bookings:** "consistent bookings", "getting leads", "random and unpredictable", "getting people in the door"

**Overwhelm:** "juggling", "no time", "all over the place", "procrastinating", "behind", "putting it off"

**Selling discomfort:** "salesy", "chasing", "not great at selling", "drop off", "ghost"

**Digital-only clients:** "digitals only", "too expensive", "defend my value", "compared on price", "selling myself short"

**Imposter syndrome:** "not good enough", "fear of being judged", "lost motivation", "giving up", "not special"

**Not knowing what to focus on:** "stuck", "where to start", "all over the place", "don't know what to focus on", "nowhere near"

**Wins language:** "work less, earn more", "ideal clients", "consistent bookings", "profitable", "freedom goal"

**Community:** "This is me too!", "Right there with you!", "I hear you!", "These are my people."

## Key Frameworks to Reference

- **The 3 Gaps:** Visibility, Message, Conversion. Which one is the real bottleneck?
- **Selling as Service:** Guiding clients from a place of service, not pushing
- **Why This, Why You, Why Now:** The three questions every offer page must answer
- **Active vs Passive Marketing:** Building both systems rather than relying on one
- **The Client Journey:** Mapping each stage from enquiry to delivery
- **Sustainable Business Scan:** Diagnosing the health of the whole business
- **3Ps Framework:** Purpose, Personality, Preview

## Internal Dialogue Map

**Pre-click:** "I am stuck again on getting consistent bookings, and I do not have all day to research this. If Ina's email can help me see the real issue and give me one thing to do, that'll be a win."

**First scan:** "What's the main idea here? Is she talking about something I am actually facing? Can I see myself in the examples?"

**Mid-read:** "Ah, so this is a visibility problem more than a pricing problem. That makes sense because my last three clients all came from word of mouth. I have no top-of-funnel system."

**At the action step:** "That task feels doable this week. I could genuinely try this before our next call."

**At the soft offer:** "This sounds like a deeper version of what she just walked me through. If I joined, I would not be starting from scratch."

**Decision to upgrade:** "Every time I read her emails or posts, I see my exact problems explained clearly and I walk away with an action I can take. If her free stuff is this useful, the paid coaching is probably the structure I need."

## Tone Notes

- Warm, direct, no-fluff
- Conversational Australian English (colour, realise, organise, enquiries)
- Gentle but honest, willing to give real feedback
- Never condescending about where someone is in their journey
- Normalises struggle without romanticising it
- Practical over inspirational, framework over motivation
- Avoids "salesy" language (the audience explicitly dislikes it)
